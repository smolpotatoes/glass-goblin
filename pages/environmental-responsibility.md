Environmental responsibility

Good news - antique glass has some pretty green credentials! Depending on its age it may have been made using coal and Victorian hard labour, but precisely because of its age its annual carbon cost is miniscule. Glass is also pretty much infinitely recyclable. Buying vintage and antique glass is always better for the environment than buying new, and the icing on the cake is that old glass tends to be of better quality than something modern that you could pick up for the same price.

I feel strongly about limiting the environmental impact my life makes on the planet, and this extends to Glass Goblin, too.


I recycle as much as possible, believing in repairing and reusing things wherever practicable. This extends to damaged and broken glass; I either repair/restore the piece, or if it’s beyond saving I repurpose the glass into jewellery and other trinkets.
I re-use packing materials wherever possible, with an emphasis on recyclable materials. Most of my cardboard boxes are donated by my local network, but whenever I run out, I collect second-hand ones from a local compressed gas company.
I choose new materials with care; using items made with recycled materials, avoiding plastics and trying to limit the distance the item travels to reach me.
This site is on Microsoft servers, who have a commitment to environmental sustainability. https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/corporate-responsibility/sustainability/report
When shipping parcels, I prefer to use Royal Mail and usually walk them to the post office or have them collected from my doorstep during the Royal Mail round. Royal Mail are the UK’s greenest delivery option for letters and parcels. https://www.royalmail.com/sustainability
All furniture for GGHQ is either antique or second hand, and sourced locally, keeping transport emissions to a minimum. My professional racking for my glass storage was sourced in Gloucestershire.
Where purchases from China etc are unavoidable, I wait until I’m able to make one larger order and ensure the items are shipped together in one go.
