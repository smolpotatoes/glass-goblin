URANIUM AND RADIUM
Is uranium glass safe?
Yes! Glass that contains uranium usually has a maxium of about 2% of the uranium compound used. Uranium mainly emits alpha radiation, and this type of radiation is easily blocked, so much so that the vast majority of ionising radiation emitted by these alpha particles will be blocked by the glass itself. The rest is weak enough to be blocked by the top layer of your skin.

What Geiger counter do you use?
My Geiger counter is a GQ GMC 300E. It is great for confirming the presence of a radioactive isotope, but less accurate for dose rates, and cannot determine which isotope is present.

Is your Geiger counter accurate?
In a nutshell; yes. But the (much) longer answer is no. My Geiger counter only measures beta, gamma and xray radiation, so does not measure alpha. For this reason, an accurate dose rate from the likes of uranium and radium isn't possible.
